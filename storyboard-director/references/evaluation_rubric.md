# Evaluation Rubric

## Purpose

Use this file to score a shot plan before calling it final.

Scores do not guarantee artistic greatness, but they do provide a gate against weak or careless output.

## Scoring scale

Use a `0-5` scale per category.

- `5.0` exceptional, strongly justified, production-ready
- `4.5` strong, clearly reasoned, minor refinements only
- `4.0` usable, but one meaningful weakness remains
- `3.0` partially working, revision needed
- `2.0` weak, misunderstanding or poor control
- `1.0` mostly ineffective
- `0.0` absent or broken

## Required categories

### `narrative_fidelity`

Does the shot serve what the script is actually expressing?

### `protagonist_priority`

Does the correct subject own the frame and emotional center?

### `blocking_clarity`

Are subject positions, movement, and relationship changes legible?

### `composition_logic`

Is the frame hierarchy intentional, readable, and non-random?

### `camera_motivation`

Is camera size, angle, and movement justified by the beat?

### `rhythm_control`

Does the timing feel emotionally correct rather than generic?

### `action_timing_readability`

Can the available shot duration clearly carry the required action chain, including setup, execution, result, and reaction when those are needed?

### `generation_controllability`

Is the prompt clean and realistic enough for Seedance-style execution?

## Overall score

Use the arithmetic mean of the required categories unless the project defines a stricter weighting.

## Release gates

- `4.5+` strong, ready to render
- `4.2-4.49` good, ship if schedule is tight
- `4.0-4.19` revise if quality matters
- `<4.0` do not finalize

## Hard fail conditions

Even with a decent average, revise if any of these are below `4.0`:

- `narrative_fidelity`
- `protagonist_priority`
- `camera_motivation`
- `action_timing_readability`
- `generation_controllability`

## Notes

Use the score as a forcing function for revision, not as self-congratulation.

If the score is high but the justification is vague, the shot is not actually verified.
