## Sound objective

Treat sound as story control, not as decoration added at the end.

For each shot, ask:

- what should the audience feel first
- what should the audience notice without looking directly
- whether the scene wants softness, wonder, intimacy, comedy, pressure, or release

If the image layer is clear but the emotional timing is weak, improve the sound layer before adding more camera complexity.

## Core sound layers

Design sound in layers. Not every shot needs every layer.

### Ambience bed

Use for place, time, scale, and air.

Examples:

- spring wind over cotton fields
- low station rain and platform hum
- quiet moon-palace chimes in the far distance

### Foley

Use for tactile actions that sell material reality.

Examples:

- cotton rustle
- soft footstep in dry soil
- bottle glass tap
- scarf fabric brushing fur

### Character micro-vocals

Use for non-dialogue character presence in cute, stylized, or creature-led pieces.

Examples:

- soft sigh
- tiny gasp
- bunny chirp
- playful cotton-creature giggle

### Stylized or magical effects

Use when the world has fantasy logic or branded magical cues.

Examples:

- osmanthus particle shimmer
- cotton bloom pop
- moonlight flow tone

### Music state

Track:

- instrumentation
- tempo energy
- emotional function
- transition point

Keep music language brief and functional.

### Brand mnemonic

Use only when the work is commercial and the brand wants a memory cue.

Examples:

- two-note soft bell mnemonic
- plush cotton puff accent on logo reveal

## Perspective and mix focus

Decide what sits in front of the mix.

Good options:

- ambience forward
- foley forward
- character reaction forward
- music forward
- logo mnemonic forward

Do not make every layer equally loud in the plan.

## Sound continuity

Track continuity across shots:

- environment bed
- musical motif
- recurring magical cue
- character vocal signature
- brand mnemonic

Abrupt changes are allowed only when the story beat justifies them.

## Seedance-specific guidance

Public Seedance material emphasizes audio-video joint generation and audio references. Use that in a controlled way.

Practical rule:

- if general mood is enough, describe sound in text
- if timing and sync matter, prepare audio references and note them in the shot plan

Text prompt sound cues should be concise:

- ambience
- one or two key foley events
- one musical state
- one signature magical or brand cue if needed

## Commercial and brand usage

For branded films:

- keep the sonic identity soft unless the brand demands harder impact
- attach the clearest mnemonic to the final reveal or emotional turn
- do not let the brand cue overpower character emotion unless the brief is explicitly product-first
