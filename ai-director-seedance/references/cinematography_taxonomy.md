## Lens feel

Prefer descriptive lens intent rather than exact focal length when the generator does not obey millimeter values reliably.

- expansive_wide
- neutral_standard
- intimate_portrait
- compressed_telephoto
- macro_detail

If a workflow benefits from explicit numbers, map them consistently:

- wide: 18-28mm
- standard: 35-50mm
- portrait: 65-85mm
- telephoto: 100mm+

## Camera movement

- locked_off
- handheld_subtle
- handheld_urgent
- slow_push_in
- dolly_in
- dolly_out
- lateral_track
- follow
- orbit
- crane_up
- crane_down
- tilt
- pan
- whip_pan
- rack_focus

Use movement to change emphasis, not as decoration.

Treat movement as valid only when it is motivated by at least one of:

- actor blocking changes the visual relationship
- the audience focus must transfer
- geography must be clarified
- emotion escalates or releases in a visible way
- a reveal or concealment depends on the camera path

If none of the above are true, default to simpler movement or a static shot.

## Blocking and staging

Describe how the subject behaves in space before placing the camera.

Track:

- where the hero starts in frame
- where supporting characters start
- who crosses frame and who holds ground
- whether the audience should read connection, distance, dominance, or support
- whether the camera follows the subject, waits for the subject, or lets the subject exit and re-enter frame

Useful blocking ideas:

- hold hero center while support enters from edge
- let the protagonist claim frame center at the turning point
- keep the helper in secondary plane when the script requires protagonist priority
- transfer the visual center only when story emphasis changes

## Composition

Describe what matters structurally:

- centered
- off_center
- symmetrical
- asymmetrical
- layered_depth
- foreground_occlusion
- negative_space
- crowd_density
- clean_background

Also decide:

- who owns the visual center at the start
- whether the visual center should transfer during the shot
- whether the frame should feel stable, pressurized, withheld, or released

## Tempo

Tempo is not just scene pacing. It is the felt timing of the shot itself.

Useful descriptors:

- withheld
- measured
- gentle
- suspended
- decisive
- accelerating
- abrupt
- floating

Tie tempo to:

- how quickly the camera starts moving
- whether the shot holds before action
- whether movement glides, snaps, or pauses
- how long the audience sits on the reaction before a cut

## Typical mappings

- intimacy -> medium close or close-up, eye level or slight profile, portrait lens feel
- vulnerability -> negative space, off-center framing, soft but directional light
- threat -> low angle or compressed framing, stronger contrast, reduced escape space
- spectacle -> wide staging, spatial depth, controlled movement
- confusion -> unstable movement, layered foreground, partial occlusion, shifting focus
- help or support -> protagonist remains primary, helper staged in secondary plane, movement follows the protagonist decision rather than the helper entrance
